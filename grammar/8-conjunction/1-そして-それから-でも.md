# そして / それから / でも - sentence-starting conjunctions

Connect two separate, complete sentences — distinct from clause-attaching particles like けど (see [[../1-particle/12-けど]]), し (see [[../1-particle/15-し]]), or から (see [[../1-particle/8a-から]]), which all glue onto the end of the *first* clause. These instead stand alone at the start of the *second* sentence, after a full stop.

## そして — "and then" (formal-leaning sequencing)
Connects two sentences in sequence, often with a sense of a notable next step — common in narration, writing, and somewhat formal/deliberate speech:

- 朝ごはんを食べた。そして、仕事に行った。— [I] ate breakfast. And then, [I] went to work.
- 頑張った。そして、成功した。— [I] worked hard. And [then], [I] succeeded.

そして can feel a little formal/narrative for very casual daily chat — in relaxed conversation, that それから below, or just starting the next sentence with nothing at all, is often more natural.

## それから — "after that" / "and then" (casual, everyday sequencing)
The everyday, casual equivalent of そして for listing what happened next, or adding one more item to a list:

- 学校に行った。それから、友達と遊んだ。— [I] went to school. After that, [I] hung out with friends.
- パンを買った。それから、牛乳も買った。— [I] bought bread. And [then I] also bought milk.

それから also works to add an afterthought item to a list, similar to "oh, and also":

- コーヒーをください。それから、水も。— Coffee, please. And also water.

## でも — "but" (standalone, sentence-initial)
The sentence-starting counterpart to clause-attached けど (see [[../1-particle/12-けど]]) — same meaning, different structural slot. でも opens a new sentence that contrasts with the one before it:

- 行きたい。でも、時間がない。— [I] want to go. But, [I] don't have time.
- 高い。でも、買った。— [It]'s expensive. But, [I] bought [it].

Compare directly with けど gluing the same idea into one sentence:
- 行きたいけど、時間がない。— [I] want to go, but [I] don't have time. (one sentence)
- 行きたい。でも、時間がない。— [I] want to go. But [I] don't have time. (two sentences, でも opens the second)

Both are common and largely interchangeable in meaning — the choice is about pacing/emphasis: でも as its own sentence gives the contrast more weight/pause than けど folded into a single breath.

## Quick comparison: clause-attached vs. sentence-starting

| Meaning | Attaches to end of clause | Starts a new sentence |
|---|---|---|
| but | 〜けど (see [[../1-particle/12-けど]]) | でも |
| and then / next | 〜て (see [[../2-verb/2-て-form]]) | そして / それから |
| because... so | 〜から (see [[../1-particle/8a-から]]) | だから (see note below) |

## Notes
- でも here is unrelated to でも as an indefinite-extending suffix (何でも — anything, 誰でも — anyone) — same spelling, different grammatical job; context makes it obvious.
- だから ("so, therefore") is the natural fourth member of this family — the sentence-starting counterpart to から, e.g. 疲れた。だから、休んだ。— [I] was tired. So, [I] rested. Worth its own note if this folder grows further.
