# えっと / えーと - thinking filler

Pure "let me think" filler — used while recalling information, deciding what to say, or working something out in real time. Unlike あの (see [[2-あの]]), which opens an interaction, えっと is used mid-thought, often when put on the spot.

## Buying time to think
Dropped in right before or during the moment of trying to remember or calculate something:

- えっと、明日は... 火曜日だ。— Um, tomorrow is... [it]'s Tuesday.
- えっと、名前なんだっけ。— Uh, what was [their] name again...
- えーと、どこまで話したっけ。— Um, where was I in the story...

## Answering a question you need a second to think about
Extremely common right after being asked something that requires a moment of recall or calculation:

- Q: 全部でいくらですか。— How much is it in total?
- A: えっと... 3000円です。— Um... it's 3000 yen.

## えっと vs あの — different jobs
えっと is about the speaker's own thinking process (internal, "let me recall/figure this out"); あの is about addressing the listener (external, "hey, before I say this..."). They can even stack: あの、えっと... — Um, uh... (hesitating both to speak up and to figure out what to say).

## Notes
- えっと/えーと is neutral register — fine in both casual and polite conversation, since it's just a natural pause, not a stylistic choice like なんか (see [[1-なんか]]).
- Lengthening the ー (えーーーと) mirrors dragging out "uhhhh" in English — more stalling, same function.
