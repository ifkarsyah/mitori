# というか - "or rather" / self-correction filler

Used to rephrase, correct, or refine what you just said (or are about to say) mid-sentence — "or rather," "I mean," "more like." Literally "or [if I were to] say" (と + 言う + か, see [[../1-particle/5a-と]] for と's quoting function).

## Rephrasing / correcting yourself mid-thought
Signals swapping out a word or framing for a more accurate one, right after saying it:

- 楽しい、というか、面白かった。— [It] was fun — or rather, interesting. (refining the word choice)
- 忙しい、というか、時間がない。— [I]'m busy — I mean, [I] don't have time. (clarifying what "busy" actually means here)

## Pushing back on a premise gently
というか can also redirect a conversation, gently suggesting the other person's framing is slightly off before offering a better one:

- A: それ、難しいね。— That's hard, huh.
- B: というか、無理じゃない? — I mean, isn't it [just] impossible? (reframing "hard" as "impossible")

## Opening with a correction to what was just implied
Common right at the start of a response, right after hearing something you want to gently push back on or reframe:

- というか、それ聞いてないんだけど。— Wait, I mean, [I] wasn't told that. (mild pushback, softened by というか instead of a blunt correction)

## というか vs まあ — different jobs
まあ (see [[6-まあ]]) hedges/downplays; というか actively rephrases or corrects. They can even combine: まあ、というか... — Well, I mean...

## Notes
- というか is casual-to-neutral register — fine in relaxed conversation, less at home in formal/polite contexts where a fuller rephrase (それより, むしろ) would be more appropriate.
- Functions similarly to inserting "I mean" or "or rather" mid-sentence in English — a live self-edit, not a planned part of the sentence structure.
