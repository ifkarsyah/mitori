# Question words — 何 / 誰 / いつ / いくら / なぜ・どうして

The core interrogatives that live outside the こそあど grid (see [[1-こそあど]] for どこ/どれ/どう/どの/どんな, which are part of that system). These are basic, load-bearing daily vocabulary — asking what, who, when, how much, and why.

## 何 (なに/なん) — what
Two readings depending on what follows — なん before certain consonants (especially な/だ/の rows, and counters), なに elsewhere. Both common, no need to overthink which to use in most cases:

- これは何ですか。— What is this? (なん before です)
- 何を食べますか。— What will [you] eat? (なに before を)
- 何時ですか。— What time is it? (なんじ)
- 何色が好きですか。— What color do [you] like? (なにいろ)

何+か/でも/も builds indefinites, already covered from the か side (see [[../1-particle/7-か]]):
- 何か食べたい。— [I] want to eat something.
- 何でもいい。— Anything's fine.
- 何もない。— There's nothing. (with negative verb)

## 誰 (だれ) / どなた — who
だれ is the standard casual/neutral form; どなた is the polite version, used with strangers or in formal contexts:

- 誰ですか。— Who is it?
- あれは誰の傘ですか。— Whose umbrella is that? (誰の — see [[../1-particle/4-の]] for の as possession)
- どなたですか。— Who may I ask is calling/speaking? (polite, e.g. answering the phone)

誰+か/も follows the same indefinite pattern as 何:
- 誰か来た。— Someone came.
- 誰もいない。— There's no one [here]. (with negative)

## いつ — when
Straightforward, no reading variation, works for both specific and general time questions:

- いつ行きますか。— When will [you] go?
- 誕生日はいつですか。— When's [your] birthday?

いつか (someday) and いつも (always) are the indefinite/extended forms, both extremely common:
- いつか日本に行きたい。— [I] want to go to Japan someday.
- 彼はいつも忙しい。— He's always busy.

## いくら — how much (price/cost)
Specifically for money/cost — not to be confused with いくつ (how many, see the counters note in [[../6-counter/1-counters]]):

- これはいくらですか。— How much is this?
- 全部でいくらになりますか。— How much does it come to in total?

## なぜ / どうして — why
Both mean "why," but どうして is the more common, natural everyday choice; なぜ leans more formal/written, or is used when the question carries a heavier, more serious weight:

- どうして遅れたの? — Why were [you] late? (casual, everyday)
- なぜですか。— Why [is that]? (more formal, or asking about something significant)

どうして also works as a genuine "how" in older/formal usage, but in modern daily conversation it's almost always "why" — for "how," see どう / どうやって below.

## どう / どうやって — how
どう (see [[1-こそあど]]'s manner row) asks about a state or opinion ("how is it"); どうやって specifically asks about method ("by what means/how do you do it"):

- 元気? どう? — How's it going? / How are you?
- これ、どうやって作るの? — How do [you] make this?
- 会社までどうやって行きますか。— How do [you] get to the office? (asking about method — train, walk, etc.)

## Quick reference

| Word | Asks about |
|---|---|
| 何 (なに/なん) | what |
| 誰 (だれ) / どなた | who |
| いつ | when |
| いくら | how much (cost) |
| いくつ | how many (see [[../6-counter/1-counters]]) |
| なぜ / どうして | why |
| どう | how (state/opinion) |
| どうやって | how (method) |
| どこ/どれ/どの/どんな | where/which/what kind (see [[1-こそあど]]) |

## Notes
- Question words never take は as their own topic marker in the question itself — they take が when they're the subject (誰が来ましたか, not 誰は), matching the new-information rule for が (see [[../1-particle/1b-が]]).
- Answers to a question-word question typically echo back with が for the same reason: 田中さんが来ました, not 田中さんは来ました, when directly answering 誰が来ましたか.
