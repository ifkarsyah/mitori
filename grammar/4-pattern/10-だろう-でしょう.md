# だろう / でしょう(でしょ) - "probably" / confirmation tag

Expresses a confident guess ("probably X") or seeks/asserts agreement with a rising tone ("...right?"). だろう is the plain-form register, でしょう the polite — でしょ is でしょう's casual clip, extremely common as a tag question.

## Forming it
Attaches after plain form (dropping だ for nouns/な-adjectives):

- 明日は晴れるだろう。— [It]'ll probably be sunny tomorrow. (plain, more common in narration/writing or masculine-leaning casual speech)
- 明日は晴れるでしょう。— [It]'ll probably be sunny tomorrow. (polite, common in weather forecasts, formal predictions)
- 彼はもう帰っただろう。— He's probably already gone home.
- 学生だろう。/ 学生でしょう。— [They]'re probably a student.

## "Probably" — confident prediction
The core use: stating a likely outcome with more confidence than かも (see [[8-かも]]), but still short of certainty:

- 田中さんは来るでしょう。— Tanaka will probably come.
- 高いだろうと思う。— I think [it]'s probably expensive. (stacks with と思う, see [[../1-particle/5a-と]])

Compare the confidence ladder: かも (might, genuinely uncertain) < かな (wondering, undecided) < だろう/でしょう (probably, fairly confident) < plain assertion (certain).

## でしょ — casual confirmation tag ("...right?")
Clipped でしょ, said with rising intonation, asks the listener to confirm or agree — similar territory to ね (see [[../1-particle/6a-ね]]), but でしょ carries more of "I'm pretty confident I'm right, back me up" rather than ね's neutral "don't you agree too":

- すごいでしょ! — Pretty amazing, right?! (showing off, expecting agreement)
- おいしいでしょ? — Tasty, right? (confident it's true, checking the listener agrees)
- だから言ったでしょ! — [That's why] I told you [so], right?! (mildly scolding — "I was right")

## でしょ vs ね — nuance difference
ね genuinely invites the listener's independent reaction/agreement ("isn't it?" — open to their own take). でしょ leans toward "I already know I'm right, confirm it" — more assertive, sometimes a little smug or playful depending on tone:

- いい天気ですね。— Nice weather, isn't it. (neutral, sharing the observation)
- いい天気でしょ? — Nice weather, right? (implying "I told you it would be" or "see, I was right")

## Notes
- だろう can sound blunt/masculine in casual speech if used carelessly — でしょ is the safer, more universally natural casual choice for the "probably/right?" role in everyday conversation.
- でしょうか is the formal, softened question form ("I wonder if... / would it be...") — distinct from casual でしょ, see the brief mention in [[../1-particle/7-か]].
