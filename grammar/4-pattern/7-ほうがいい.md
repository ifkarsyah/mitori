# ほうがいい - "should" / "better to"

The standard way to give advice or state a preference between options — "[you] should do X" / "it's better to do X." One of the most commonly used advice-giving patterns in daily conversation.

## Forming it — affirmative advice
Plain past (た-form, see [[2-て-form]] in 2-verb) + ほうがいい, even though the meaning is about future/hypothetical action, not something already done:

- 早く寝たほうがいいよ。— [You] should sleep early. (lit. "the having-slept-early option is better")
- 病院に行ったほうがいいですよ。— [You] should go to the hospital.
- もっと勉強したほうがいい。— [You] should study more.

Using た-form here (not plain non-past 寝るほうがいい) is the standard, most natural pattern for advice about a specific, immediate situation — using plain non-past instead sounds more like a general statement of preference than concrete advice.

## Forming it — negative advice ("shouldn't")
Plain negative (ない-form, see [[3-plain-vs-polite-form]]) + ほうがいい — note: **not** た-form here, unlike the affirmative:

- お酒を飲まないほうがいいですよ。— [You] shouldn't drink alcohol.
- 無理しないほうがいい。— [You] shouldn't push [yourself] too hard.
- 心配しないほうがいいよ。— [You] shouldn't worry.

This affirmative-uses-た / negative-uses-ない asymmetry is the one thing worth memorizing carefully — mixing them up (using た-form for negative advice) is a common mistake.

## General preference (comparing two options)
Beyond advice to someone, ほうがいい also states a plain preference between two things, tying back to より (see [[3-comparison]] in 3-adjective):

- コーヒーよりお茶のほうがいい。— Tea is better than coffee. (preference, not advice)
- 静かなほうがいいです。— [I] prefer [it] quiet. (な-adjective + な + ほうがいい)

## Softening the directness
ほうがいい can sound quite direct/opinionated on its own — よ or と思う often soften it into a suggestion rather than a command:

- 早く寝たほうがいいと思う。— I think [you] should sleep early. (softer, framed as opinion — see [[../1-particle/5a-と]] for と思う)
- 行ったほうがいいんじゃない? — Isn't it better to go? (very casual, softened via a rhetorical question — see [[1-んです]] for the んじゃない construction)

## Notes
- ほうがいい compares an implied alternative — even without stating it, 早く寝たほうがいい implies "...rather than staying up."
- Don't confuse this ほう (方, "direction/option/side") with 方 as in やり方 ("way of doing") — related etymologically but functioning differently; here ほう always pairs with が+いい to form the comparison structure.
