# んじゃない(?) - rhetorical "isn't it?" tag

Floats an opinion or observation as a soft rhetorical question rather than a flat statement — "isn't it...?", "I think it's..., no?" Built from の/ん (see [[../1-particle/4-の]]) + じゃない (casual negative copula, see [[../2-verb/3-plain-vs-polite-form]]), but the negative here isn't literal — it's a rhetorical-question shape, not an actual negation.

## Forming it
Plain form + んじゃない (casual) / んじゃないですか (polite):

- それ、いいんじゃない? — Isn't that good? / I think that's good, no?
- 高いんじゃない? — Isn't [that] expensive?
- 行ったほうがいいんじゃない? — Isn't it better to go? (stacks with ほうがいい, see [[7-ほうがいい]])
- 大丈夫なんじゃないですか。— Isn't [it] probably fine? (polite version, な inserted for na-adjectives/nouns before ん, same as んです — see [[1-んです]])

## Not a real negative — a softened assertion
The literal grammar reads as "isn't it not X" (double negative-shaped), but the actual meaning is a gently floated positive opinion — the rhetorical question shape exists specifically to avoid stating something too bluntly:

- それ、変じゃない? — Isn't that weird? (= I think that's weird, but framed as a question so it's less confrontational)
- できるんじゃない? — Can't [you] probably do it? (= I think you can, encouraging)

## んじゃない vs でしょ vs ね — three ways to float an opinion softly
All three invite some kind of listener reaction, but with different flavors:

| Form | Flavor |
|---|---|
| 〜んじゃない? | "I think X, but I'm floating it as a question" — genuinely open to pushback |
| 〜でしょ? | "I'm confident X is right, confirm it" — see [[10-だろう-でしょう]] |
| 〜ね | "I assume you agree, sharing the feeling" — see [[../1-particle/6a-ね]] |

んじゃない is the most tentative/hedge-y of the three — it's specifically useful when suggesting something without wanting to sound like you're asserting a fact.

## Common daily uses: suggestions and gentle pushback
- 今日は疲れてるし、休んだほうがいいんじゃない? — [You]'re tired today, maybe [you] should rest? (a suggestion, softened)
- それ、違うんじゃない? — Isn't that wrong? / I don't think that's right. (gentle correction, less blunt than 違うよ)

## Notes
- Intonation matters — rising, soft intonation reads as genuinely open/gentle; flatter delivery can sound more like a mild assertion of disagreement, closer to "that's not right, is it."
- んじゃないかな combines this with かな (see [[9-かな]]) for maximum softness/hedging: 大丈夫なんじゃないかな。— I think it's probably okay, though [I'm not totally sure].
