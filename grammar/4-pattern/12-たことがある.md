# たことがある - "have done X before" (experience)

States that you have experience doing something at some point in the (usually unspecified) past — "I've done X before," "I have experience with X." Extremely common in daily conversation about travel, food, hobbies, and general life experience.

## Forming it
Plain past (た-form, see [[../2-verb/2-て-form]]) + ことがある:

- 日本に行ったことがあります。— [I]'ve been to Japan [before].
- 納豆を食べたことがある。— [I]'ve eaten natto before.
- 富士山に登ったことがありますか。— Have [you] ever climbed Mt. Fuji?

Negative — "never done X":
- 海外に行ったことがない。— [I]'ve never been abroad.
- そんなの聞いたことがありません。— [I]'ve never heard of such a thing.

## Emphasis on experience, not recency or a specific time
This pattern is specifically about the *fact of having done something at least once*, without pinning down when — contrast with plain past, which reports a specific event:

- 日本に行きました。— [I] went to Japan. (a specific, completed trip — implies a known "when")
- 日本に行ったことがあります。— [I]'ve been to Japan [at some point]. (experience/résumé fact, timing irrelevant)

Don't use this pattern for something that just happened or has an obvious specific timeframe — たことがある sounds odd for very recent/specific events (誰かと今日話したことがある sounds strange; just 今日話しました is natural).

## Asking about experience — common conversation starter
〜たことがありますか is one of the most natural ways to make small talk about travel, food, or hobbies:

- 韓国に行ったことがありますか。— Have [you] ever been to Korea?
- この映画、見たことある? — Have [you] seen this movie [before]? (casual, ある without ですか)

## Casual clipping
In relaxed speech, ことがある often drops the が: ことある — very common, natural contraction:

- それ、聞いたことある。— [I]'ve heard of that.
- やったことない。— [I]'ve never done [it].

## Notes
- Not to be confused with things happening habitually (that's ている/たり〜たりする, see [[../2-verb/10-たり]]) — たことがある is strictly about whether an experience exists at all in your history, once or more, not how often.
- こと here nominalizes the verb (turning "did X" into "the experience of having done X") — the same こと used in 〜ことができる (potential, see [[../2-verb/5-potential-form]]).
