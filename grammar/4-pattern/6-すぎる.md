# すぎる - "too much"

Attaches to verbs or adjectives to say something is excessive — "too [X]," "[verb] too much." Extremely common in daily complaints, observations, and self-deprecating remarks.

## Forming it

**With verbs** — ます-stem (see [[1-conjugation-group]] in 2-verb) + すぎる:

- 食べる → 食べすぎる — eat too much
- 飲む → 飲みすぎる — drink too much
- 働く → 働きすぎる — work too much
- 寝る → 寝すぎる — sleep too much

**With い-adjectives** — drop the final い, add すぎる:

- 高い → 高すぎる — too expensive
- 難しい → 難しすぎる — too difficult
- 忙しい → 忙しすぎる — too busy

**With な-adjectives** — drop な, add すぎる:

- 静か(な) → 静かすぎる — too quiet
- 便利(な) → 便利すぎる — too convenient

Irregular: いい → よすぎる (not いすぎる).

The result conjugates as a る-verb from there: すぎる/すぎない/すぎた/すぎました.

## Core use — excess, usually with a negative connotation
Almost always implies "more than is good/appropriate," even when the underlying thing is positive:

- 昨日、食べすぎました。— [I] ate too much yesterday. (mild regret)
- これ、高すぎる! — This is way too expensive!
- 頑張りすぎないでください。— Please don't overdo it [with effort]. (see [[2-requests]] for the ないでください pattern)

## Combining with other patterns
すぎる pairs naturally with the casual regret contraction ちゃう (see [[4-ちゃう]]) and with んです (see [[1-んです]]) when explaining why something's a problem:

- 食べすぎちゃった。— [I] ended up eating too much (oops).
- ちょっと働きすぎなんです。— [The thing is,] [I]'m working a bit too much.

## Notes
- すぎる can be nominalized with 食べ過ぎ (as a noun: "overeating") — common in health/lifestyle contexts: 食べ過ぎは体に悪い。— Overeating is bad for [your] body.
- Distinct from とても/すごく ("very") — すぎる specifically frames something as *excessive/undesirable*, not just intense. とても高い (very expensive, neutral intensity) vs 高すぎる (too expensive, implying it's a problem).
