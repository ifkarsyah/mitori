# ちゃう / じゃう - casual contraction of てしまう

Casual, contracted form of て-form + しまう (see [[2-て-form]] in 2-verb). てしまう itself means "end up doing," "finish doing completely," or "do something regrettable" — ちゃう is how that sounds in relaxed daily speech, and it's everywhere in real conversation.

## How the contraction works
て + しまう → ちゃう (for verbs whose て-form ends in て). で + しまう → じゃう (for verbs whose て-form ends in で, i.e. the んで group — see the sound-shift table in [[2-て-form]]):

- 食べてしまう → 食べちゃう — end up eating / eat it all
- 忘れてしまう → 忘れちゃう — end up forgetting
- 読んでしまう → 読んじゃう — end up reading / finish reading (で→じゃう)
- 死んでしまう → 死んじゃう — end up dying

Conjugates as a regular う-verb from there: ちゃう/ちゃった/ちゃわない, じゃう/じゃった/じゃわない.

## Two core meanings (same as full てしまう)

**Completion — did something fully/entirely:**
- もう食べちゃった。— [I] already finished eating (all of it).
- 全部読んじゃった。— [I] read the whole thing.

**Regret / unintended consequence — something happened that you wish hadn't, often accidentally:**
- 財布を忘れちゃった。— [I] went and forgot my wallet. (oops)
- 壊しちゃった。— [I] broke it. (accidentally, and feeling bad about it)
- 寝ちゃった。— [I] [accidentally] fell asleep. (e.g. when you meant to stay awake)

Which reading applies is mostly determined by context — a completion-type verb with no obvious downside (食べちゃう) reads as "finished eating"; an accident-prone context (忘れちゃう, 壊しちゃう) reads as regret.

## Very common standalone reactions
- やっちゃった! — [I] [screwed up and] did it! / Oh no, I did the thing [again]. (very common exclamation after a small mistake)
- しちゃった。— [I] did it [without meaning to] / [I] went ahead and did it.

## Politeness note
ちゃう/じゃう is casual-register only — for polite speech, use the full てしまいます/てしまいました, or drop the nuance and just use a plain verb if the "regret/completion" flavor isn't essential to convey.

## Notes
- Don't confuse the contraction ちゃう with random verbs that happen to end in ちゃう unrelatedly — in practice this is rare, context makes it obvious.
- Casual speech often further contracts the past tense: ちゃった is already the norm; you won't typically see it shortened further, but you will hear it constantly in fast, natural speech (やっちゃった、忘れちゃった、寝ちゃった are all near-reflexive daily phrases).
