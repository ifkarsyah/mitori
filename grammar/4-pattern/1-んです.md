# んです / んだ - explanatory nuance

Adds an "explaining/seeking explanation" undertone to a sentence — signals "here's the reason/context," or asks "what's the story here?" Extremely common in natural spoken Japanese despite being rarely taught early. んです is the polite register, んだ the plain/casual register (see [[3-plain-vs-polite-form]] in 2-verb). Built on plain form + の (see [[4-の]] in 1-particle, "explanatory の"), contracted to ん.

## Forming it
Attaches to the plain form of verbs/adjectives, and to noun/な-adjective + な (not だ) before んです:

- 食べる → 食べるんです / 食べるんだ
- 食べた → 食べたんです / 食べたんだ
- 忙しい → 忙しいんです / 忙しいんだ
- 学生 → 学生なんです / 学生なんだ (な inserted before ん for nouns/な-adjectives — unlike plain だ, which is dropped here)

## Explaining a situation (giving background/reason)
The core use: framing a statement as background context or a reason for something, rather than a flat, isolated fact:

- 疲れたんです。— [The thing is,] I'm tired. (explaining why you look tired, or why you're asking to leave early)
- 電車に乗り遅れたんです。— [I'm explaining:] I missed the train. (as opposed to bare 電車に乗り遅れました, which just states the fact with no implied connection to anything)

Compare: 疲れました is a neutral statement. 疲れたんです implies "and that's relevant to what's going on / why I'm telling you this."

## Seeking an explanation (question form)
んですか asks "what's the story?" — genuinely curious, more probing than a bare か-question:

- どうしたんですか。— What's wrong? / What happened? (noticing something's off, asking for the story)
- 何を見ているんですか。— What are [you] looking at? (curious about something already observed, not a cold opening question)

Compare: 何を見ますか (a neutral, out-of-nowhere question about future viewing) vs 何を見ているんですか (noticing someone is currently looking at something, asking about it).

## Softening a request or opinion (with けど/が)
んです pairs constantly with the softening けど/が (see [[12-けど]] in 1-particle) to lead into a request or opinion gently, without stating it too bluntly:

- ちょっと聞きたいんですけど。— [I] wanted to ask something... (leads into a question softly)
- これ、いいと思うんですが。— I think this is good, but... (offering an opinion, leaving room for pushback)

## Casual んだ
Plain-register んだ works identically, just less formal — extremely common in casual conversation, inner monologue, and casual writing:

- そうなんだ! — Oh, I see! / Really! (a very common reaction to new information)
- 疲れたんだ。— [Explaining, casually:] I'm tired.

そうなんだ/そうなんですね are near-automatic reactions in casual/polite conversation respectively — worth having ready as reflexes.

## Notes
- Overusing んです on every sentence sounds unnatural — it's for when there's genuinely some explanation, context, or noticing involved, not a default sentence-ender.
- んです often gets contracted further in very casual speech to just んだ or even dropped to plain の (see [[4-の]] in 1-particle) — all closely related, same underlying mechanic.
