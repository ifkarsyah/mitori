# Requests — て / てください / ないでください / お願いします

How to ask someone to do something, or ask them *not* to — a survival-essential set of patterns, all built on て-form (see [[2-て-form]] in 2-verb), plus お願いします for requesting things rather than actions.

## Casual request — bare て-form
Just the て-form on its own, often with rising intonation, works as a soft casual request or instruction between friends/family:

- ちょっと待って。— Wait a sec.
- これ、取って。— Grab that [for me].
- 手伝って! — Help [me]!

This is not rude — it's the normal way to ask close friends/family for small things. It would sound too blunt with strangers or superiors, where てください or further softening is expected.

## Polite request — てください
The standard, safe polite request — works in most everyday polite situations (asking a stranger, service staff, coworkers):

- ちょっと待ってください。— Please wait a moment.
- こちらに来てください。— Please come this way.
- もう一度言ってください。— Please say that again.

てください can still sound like a direct instruction in some contexts (closer to "please do X" as a directive) — for a softer, more deferential request (especially to someone senior or in a formal setting), see the ていただけますか note below.

## Negative request — ないでください
"Please don't [do X]" — built on the plain negative (see [[3-plain-vs-polite-form]] in 2-verb) + でください:

- 写真を撮らないでください。— Please don't take photos.
- ここに座らないでください。— Please don't sit here.
- 心配しないでください。— Please don't worry.

Casually, just ないで on its own (dropping ください) works among friends:

- 心配しないで。— Don't worry. (softer, no explicit ください needed)
- 行かないで! — Don't go!

## お願いします — requesting a thing/service, not an action
Where てください attaches to a *verb* ("please do X"), お願いします attaches to a *noun* — "please [handle/give me] X." Standard for ordering, requesting items, or asking for a service:

- 水をお願いします。— Water, please.
- 会計をお願いします。— The check, please.
- 予約をお願いします。— [I'd like to] make a reservation, please.

For simple physical items, ください and お願いします overlap and are roughly interchangeable — 水をください and 水をお願いします both work, with お願いします feeling a shade more polite/service-oriented:

- 水をください。— [Give me] water, please. (direct, request for the object itself)
- 水をお願いします。— Water, please. (slightly softer/more formal framing, common when ordering)

## お願いします standing alone
Unlike ください, お願いします can stand completely alone, without a preceding verb or even a noun — used when the request is already obvious from context, or as a general "please [take care of this]":

- (handing over a form) お願いします。— Here you go, please [handle this]. / Please [take care of it].
- こちらへどうぞ。— Right this way. / (客) お願いします。— (customer, following) Thank you / please [lead the way].

## よろしくお願いします — the essential set phrase
お願いします is the base of よろしくお願いします, one of the most common phrases in Japanese daily life — said when starting a working relationship, asking a favor, or closing an introduction. It has no direct English equivalent and no ください counterpart:

- はじめまして、よろしくお願いします。— Nice to meet you, [I look forward to working with you / please treat me well].
- 今日はよろしくお願いします。— [Let's do our best] today. (said before a meeting, lesson, collaborative task)

## More polite / deferential requests
For situations calling for extra politeness (asking a favor of someone senior, a customer-facing request, formal writing), てもらえますか or ていただけますか are common upgrades from てください — literally asking "could I receive the favor of your doing X?" (see [[8-giving-receiving]] in 2-verb for the もらう mechanic behind this):

- 手伝ってもらえますか。— Could [you] help me? (fairly polite)
- もう少し待っていただけますか。— Could [you] wait a bit longer? (more formal/deferential)

These read as questions asking permission/favor rather than direct commands, which is why they land as more polite than a bare てください.

## Quick ladder (casual → most polite)

| Form | Register | Attaches to |
|---|---|---|
| 〜て | Casual, close friends/family | verb |
| 〜てください | Standard polite, safe default | verb |
| 〜てもらえますか | Polite, asking a favor | verb |
| 〜ていただけますか | Formal/deferential | verb |
| Xをお願いします | Standard polite, ordering/requesting items | noun |

Negative mirrors the same ladder: 〜ないで (casual) → 〜ないでください (polite).

## Notes
- てください directed at a stranger out of nowhere for something non-obvious can sound like an order — pairing with すみません or a softening んですが (see [[1-んです]]) makes it land more naturally: すみません、写真を撮ってもらえますか。— Excuse me, could [you] take a photo [for me]?
- Don't confuse ないでください (please don't do X) with なくてもいい (don't have to / it's fine not to do X, the negative counterpart of permission てもいい) — different meanings, easy to mix up at a glance.
