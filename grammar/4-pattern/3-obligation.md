# Obligation — 〜なければならない / 〜なくちゃ / 〜なきゃ

"Have to / must [do X]" — built from the plain negative (see [[3-plain-vs-polite-form]] in 2-verb) plus a conditional-shaped ending. Several versions exist on a formality spectrum from stiff/written to very casual.

## The underlying logic
All versions share the same shape: [plain negative stem]く + a conditional ending ("if [you] don't do X..."), with the rest ("...it's no good / won't do") dropped or contracted. Literally "if [you] don't do X, [it's bad]" → practically "[you] have to do X."

## Formal/written: 〜なければならない
ない-form, drop い, add ければならない:

- 行く → 行かない → 行かなければならない — [I] have to go.
- 食べる → 食べない → 食べなければならない — [I] have to eat.
- する → しない → しなければならない — [I] have to do [it].

This is the full, formal version — common in writing, announcements, rules. A bit stiff for casual daily speech but not wrong.

## Slightly softer/common: 〜なくてはいけない
Same idea, different conditional ending (なくては instead of なければ), also fairly common in both speech and writing:

- 行かなくてはいけない — [I] have to go.
- 食べなくてはいけない — [I] have to eat.

なければならない and なくてはいけない are largely interchangeable in daily use — pick whichever comes to mind.

## Casual contraction: 〜なくちゃ
なくては contracts to なくちゃ in casual speech — the ending (いけない) is very often dropped entirely, since the obligation is understood from なくちゃ alone:

- 行かなくちゃ(いけない)。— [I] gotta go.
- もう寝なくちゃ。— [I] gotta sleep now.

Dropping いけない and just ending on なくちゃ is extremely natural and common — a bare なくちゃ! by itself (e.g. glancing at a clock) reads as "gotta go/gotta do it."

## Most casual contraction: 〜なきゃ
なければ contracts to なきゃ — same deal, いけない/ならない routinely dropped:

- 行かなきゃ(ならない)。— [I] gotta go.
- 早く起きなきゃ。— [I] have to get up early.

なきゃ is probably the single most common way "have to" gets expressed in relaxed daily conversation — more casual-sounding than なくちゃ to most ears, though both are fine.

## Quick formality reference

| Form | Register |
|---|---|
| 〜なければならない | Formal/written |
| 〜なくてはいけない | Neutral/common |
| 〜なくちゃ(いけない) | Casual, contraction of なくては |
| 〜なきゃ(ならない) | Very casual, contraction of なければ |

## Notes
- All of these attach to the plain negative stem — the group-dependent negative conjugation rules from [[1-conjugation-group]] (2-verb) apply first, before adding these endings.
- 〜なきゃ/なくちゃ standing completely alone (no verb before it, no いけない after) is a common standalone reaction meaning "I should get going" or "gotta do that [thing we were just discussing]" — context fills in the rest.
