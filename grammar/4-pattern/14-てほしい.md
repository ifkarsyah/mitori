# てほしい - "want someone [else] to do X"

Expresses wanting another person to do something — the other-directed counterpart to たい (see [[../2-verb/4-たい-form]]), which is strictly about the speaker's own desire to act.

## Forming it
て-form (see [[../2-verb/2-て-form]]) + ほしい, which conjugates as a regular い-adjective:

- 来てほしい。— [I] want [you] to come.
- 手伝ってほしいんですが。— [I]'d like [you] to help, if [you] could. (stacking with んです, see [[1-んです]], for a softer request-flavored statement)
- もっと話してほしかった。— [I] wish [he]'d talked more. (past: てほしかった)

Negative — wanting someone *not* to do something:
- 心配してほしくない。— [I] don't want [you] to worry.
- もう来てほしくない。— [I] don't want [you] to come anymore.

## たい vs てほしい — who's doing the action
This is the one thing to keep straight: たい attaches when the *speaker* is the one who wants to act; てほしい attaches when the speaker wants *someone else* to act:

- 行きたい。— [I] want to go. (I'm the one going)
- 行ってほしい。— [I] want [you] to go. (someone else is going, I want it to happen)

Mixing these up is a common learner slip, since English "want" doesn't grammaticalize this distinction the same way.

## てほしい vs a direct request (てください)
てほしい states a desire/wish about someone else's action — it's more of an expressed hope than an actual command, and often softer/more indirect than directly asking with てください (see [[2-requests]]):

- 手伝ってください。— Please help [me]. (direct request)
- 手伝ってほしいな。— [I] wish [you'd] help [me]. (expressing a wish, softer, sometimes used when not directly asking, just voicing a hope)

てほしい is common when talking *about* wanting something from someone who isn't present, or when softening a wish rather than issuing an instruction.

## Notes
- Like たい, てほしい directly describes the speaker's own feeling — for describing a third party's wish about someone else, hedge with と思っている or がっている constructions (long-tail, not needed early — see the third-person note in [[../2-verb/4-たい-form]] for the parallel logic with たい).
- Often paired with な/よね for a musing, wishful tone in casual speech: 早く終わってほしいな。— [I] wish it would end soon.
