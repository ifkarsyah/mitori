# Verb-ない? - casual invitation

A plain negative question used specifically to invite someone to do something together — "wanna...?", "won't you...?" One of the most natural, common ways to propose plans casually, distinct from んじゃない's rhetorical-opinion use (see [[11-んじゃない]]).

## Forming it
Plain negative (see [[../2-verb/3-plain-vs-polite-form]]) + rising intonation, written with a question mark in casual text:

- 一緒に行かない? — Wanna go together?
- お茶しない? — Wanna grab tea?
- 今度、映画見ない? — Wanna watch a movie sometime?
- 週末、遊ばない? — Wanna hang out this weekend?

## Why negative form for a positive invitation
The negative framing ("won't you do X?") softens the invitation compared to a flat positive suggestion — it leaves room for the other person to say no without it feeling like they're rejecting a direct proposal. This mirrors ませんか as its polite counterpart (see the note in [[../2-verb/7-volitional-form]]):

- 行かない? — Wanna go? (casual, plain negative)
- 行きませんか。— Won't [you] go [with me]? (polite negative — same softening logic, formal register)

Both are gentler than a bare volitional 行こう (let's go — see [[../2-verb/7-volitional-form]]), which reads more like a decision already being made rather than an open invitation.

## Responding to an invitation
Natural casual responses:

- いいね、行こう! — Sounds good, let's go! (accepting, switching to volitional)
- うん、いいよ。— Yeah, sure.
- ごめん、今日はちょっと... — Sorry, today's a bit... (declining, trailing off — see [[../5-filler/5-ちょっと]] for this soft-refusal pattern)

## Distinguishing from a real negative question
Context and intonation separate this from an actual "aren't you doing X?" question — an invitation-ない? is warm/forward-looking (proposing something new), while a literal negative question usually checks on an existing plan or fact:

- 行かない? — Wanna go? (proposing something new)
- 行かないの? — [You]'re not going? (checking on an already-known plan, mild surprise — see the の nuance in [[../1-particle/4-の]])

## Notes
- This pattern works with any verb, not just social activities — 食べない? (want to eat [some]?), 座らない? (wanna sit?) — anywhere you'd propose a shared action.
- Purely casual register — for anyone you'd need politeness with, use ませんか instead.
