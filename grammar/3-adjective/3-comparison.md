# Comparison — もっと / より / 一番

Japanese doesn't conjugate adjectives for comparison (no "-er"/"-est" equivalent) — comparison is done with separate words layered on top of the plain adjective from [[1-i-vs-na-adjective]].

## より — "more than" (the comparison particle)
より marks what something is being compared against — attaches after the noun being compared to:

- 今日は昨日より暑いです。— Today is hotter than yesterday. (attached to 昨日, the thing compared against)
- 電車はバスより速い。— The train is faster than the bus.
- これはそれよりいいです。— This is better than that.

The compared item doesn't need を/は/が — より takes its place as the marker on the "than X" side.

## もっと — "more" (intensifier, no explicit comparison target)
もっと boosts a quality without necessarily naming what it's being compared to — "more," "even more":

- もっと大きいのがほしい。— [I] want a bigger one. (bigger [than what I have], target implied not stated)
- もっと頑張ってください。— Please try harder [than you are now].
- もっと静かにしてください。— Please be quieter.

もっと and より can combine: これはそれよりもっといい。— This is even better than that. (more emphatic layering)

## 一番 — "the most" (superlative)
一番 (literally "number one") marks the top of a group — Japan's equivalent of "-est":

- この中で一番好きです。— [I] like [this one] the most out of these.
- 富士山は日本で一番高い山です。— Mt. Fuji is the tallest mountain in Japan.
- 一番好きな食べ物は何ですか。— What's [your] favorite (most-liked) food?

一番 typically pairs with で to mark the group/category being compared within: [category]で一番[adjective].

## Asking "which is more...?"
どちら/どっち (which of two) + が + adjective + ですか is the standard comparison question:

- コーヒーと紅茶とどちらが好きですか。— Which do [you] like more, coffee or tea?
- どっちが安い? — Which one's cheaper? (casual)

For three or more options, どれ (which, of three+) replaces どちら:

- この中でどれが一番おいしいですか。— Which one [of these] is the tastiest?

## Equal comparison — 〜と同じくらい
"As X as Y" / "about the same as":

- 兄は父と同じくらい背が高い。— [My] older brother is about as tall as [my] father.

## Quick reference

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---|---|
| AはBより[adj] | A is more [adj] than B |
| もっと[adj] | more [adj] (no explicit target) |
| [category]で一番[adj] | the most [adj] in [category] |
| AとBとどちらが[adj]ですか | which is more [adj], A or B? |
| Aと同じくらい[adj] | as [adj] as A |

## Notes
- より on its own (without もっと) is the neutral, common way to compare two things — don't feel like you need もっと every time; もっと is for emphasis or when there's no explicit "than X" target in the sentence.
- な-adjectives and い-adjectives both slot into these patterns exactly as their plain form — no special comparison conjugation to learn beyond what's already in [[1-i-vs-na-adjective]].
