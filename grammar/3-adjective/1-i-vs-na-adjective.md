# い-adjective vs な-adjective

Japanese adjectives split into two structurally different types. Telling them apart matters because they conjugate completely differently — い-adjectives conjugate on their own, な-adjectives lean on the copula だ/です just like nouns (see [[3-plain-vs-polite-form]] in 2-verb).

## Identifying the two types

**い-adjectives** — end in い in dictionary form (with a few exceptions below), conjugate independently:

- 高い (takai) — expensive/tall
- 暑い (atsui) — hot
- おいしい (oishii) — tasty
- いい (ii) / 良い (yoi) — good (irregular, see below)

**な-adjectives** — don't end in a "real" い (or do, but aren't true い-adjectives — see the trap below), require な before a noun and だ/です for predicate use, behave grammatically like nouns:

- 静か(な) — quiet
- 元気(な) — energetic/well
- きれい(な) — pretty/clean
- 有名(な) — famous
- 好き(な) — liked/favorite (note: きれい and 嫌い/好き end in い but are な-adjectives — the trap below)

## The い-adjective trap
A handful of words end in -い but are actually な-adjectives — memorize these as exceptions:

- きれい(な) — pretty (not きれ + い)
- 嫌い(な) — disliked
- 有名(な) is safe (doesn't end in い) but きらい/きれい specifically are the classic gotchas.

Test: try dropping the い and conjugating as an い-adjective (静かい→静かくない). If that sounds wrong and you'd naturally reach for だ/です instead, it's な.

## い-adjective conjugation
Conjugates without needing だ/です at all in plain form:

| | Plain | Polite |
|---|---|---|
| Non-past affirmative | 高い | 高いです |
| Non-past negative | 高くない | 高くないです / 高くありません |
| Past affirmative | 高かった | 高かったです |
| Past negative | 高くなかった | 高くなかったです |

Pattern: drop い, then -くない (negative), -かった (past), -くなかった (past negative). This shift (い→く/かった) is worth drilling the same way the て-form sound shifts are.

## な-adjective conjugation
Conjugates exactly like a noun + copula (だ/です) — see the copula table in [[3-plain-vs-polite-form]]:

| | Plain | Polite |
|---|---|---|
| Non-past affirmative | 静かだ | 静かです |
| Non-past negative | 静かじゃない | 静かじゃないです / 静かではありません |
| Past affirmative | 静かだった | 静かでした |
| Past negative | 静かじゃなかった | 静かじゃなかったです |

## Modifying a noun (attributive use)
This is where the two types visibly diverge in name:

- い-adjective + noun: nothing extra needed — 高い本 (expensive book)
- な-adjective + な + noun: な is required — 静かな部屋 (quiet room), not 静か部屋

Dropping な before a noun is one of the most common beginner mistakes with な-adjectives.

## いい — the one irregular い-adjective
いい (good) conjugates using its older form 良い (よい) for everything except the plain non-past affirmative:

- いい (not いくない for negative)
- 良くない (よくない) — not good
- 良かった (よかった) — was good / thank goodness
- 良くなかった (よくなかった) — was not good

良かった as a standalone exclamation ("thank goodness," "glad [that happened]") is extremely common in daily speech — worth knowing on its own.

## Notes
- Both types can be nominalized/turned adverbial — see [[2-adverbial-form]].
- な-adjectives are sometimes listed in dictionaries without な (静か, 元気) since な itself isn't part of the word — only added when directly modifying a noun.
