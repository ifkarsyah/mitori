# で - location-of-action / means particle

で marks the setting an action takes place in, or the tool/method used to do it. See [[3a-に]] for the に-vs-で comparison.

## Location of action
When a verb describes something happening (not just existing), で marks where it happens:

- 公園で遊びます。— [I] play in the park.
- レストランで食べました。— [I] ate at a restaurant.
- 図書館で勉強します。— [I] study at the library.

Compare with いる/ある, which take に instead — see the split below.

## Means / instrument
で marks the tool, method, or medium used to do something — "by means of," "with," "using":

- 電車で行きます。— [I] go by train.
- はしで食べます。— [I] eat with chopsticks.
- 日本語で話しました。— [I] spoke in Japanese.
- ペンで書いてください。— Please write with a pen.

## Cause/reason
で can mark the cause behind a state or event, especially with natural causes or circumstances:

- 風邪で休みました。— [I] took the day off because of a cold.
- 事故で電車が止まった。— The train stopped due to an accident.

## Scope/limit ("within X")
で can mark a bounding quantity, time limit, or group:

- 3人で行きます。— [We] go as a group of 3.
- 1時間でできます。— [It] can be done within an hour.
- 全部で1000円です。— It's 1000 yen in total.

## に vs で — the key split
The classic beginner trap is choosing between に and で for "at/in [place]." They split along **existence vs. action**:

| で | に |
|---|---|
| Where an **action happens** | Where something **exists** (いる/ある) |
| Means/instrument of doing something | Fixed point in time |
| — | Destination of motion |
| 部屋で勉強します。(studying — an action happening in the room) | 部屋にいます。(existing — just being in the room) |

A useful test: can you meaningfully ask "doing what, there?" — if yes, で. If the sentence is really just answering "where is it," そこにある/いる territory — に.

Some verbs genuinely take either depending on what you mean: 公園に集まる (gather at — arriving at a point) vs 公園で集まって遊ぶ (gather and play — action happening there). See [[3a-に]] for the full breakdown from に's side.

## Notes
- で also marks the material something is made from: 木で作った椅子。— A chair made of wood.
- で can stack after topic/contrast は: 電車では行きません。— [I] won't go by train (implying some other way).
