# と - "and/with" (people, exhaustive lists) particle

Links nouns exhaustively ("and"), or marks a companion/counterpart for an action done together. See [[5b-も]] for how と differs from も's "also" role.

## Exhaustive "and" (complete list)
と lists all the items — nothing left out. Contrast with や (not covered in the core set), which implies "and others":

- パンとコーヒーを買いました。— I bought bread and coffee. (only those two things)
- 犬と猫がいます。— There's a dog and a cat.

## "With" (companion)
と marks who you do something together with — the co-participant, not the object:

- 友達と映画を見ました。— I watched a movie with a friend.
- 一人で行くより、家族と行きたい。— Rather than going alone, I want to go with family.

Compare: 友達に会いました (I met [ran into] a friend, に marks the target) vs 友達と会いました (I met up with a friend, と marks doing it together, more planned/mutual).

## Quoting / reporting speech and thought
と marks the content of what's said, thought, called, or written — pairs constantly with 言う/思う/聞く:

- 「行きます」と言いました。— [He] said, "I'll go."
- 明日雨が降ると思います。— I think it'll rain tomorrow.
- これは日本語で「猫」と言います。— This is called "neko" in Japanese.

This is the と behind the workhorse pattern 〜と思う (see [[0-overview]]) — always plain form before と思う, even in polite sentences.

## Conditional "if/when" (と-conditional)
と can form a conditional meaning "whenever X happens, Y naturally follows" — mechanical/automatic relationships, not one-off choices:

- 春になると、花が咲きます。— When spring comes, flowers bloom.
- このボタンを押すと、電気がつきます。— If you press this button, the light turns on.

Less flexible than たら for everyday hypothetical situations — see the note on たら in [[0-overview]] for why that's usually the better default.

## Reciprocal verbs
Certain verbs describing mutual action pair naturally with と since the action inherently involves two sides:

- 彼と結婚しました。— [I] married him.
- 隣の人と喧嘩した。— [I] had a fight with my neighbor.

## Notes
- と can't be dropped the way を is — it's load-bearing for clarity in "and" lists and quoting.
- と can stack with は for contrast: 彼とは話しません。— I won't talk with him [implying: unlike others].
