# から - "from" / "because" particle

Two distinct jobs: marking a starting point (time/place), and marking a reason/cause. They look identical but attach to different things — see [[8b-まで]] for the "until" counterpart that pairs with から's starting-point use.

## Starting point: time/place "from"
Marks where or when something begins:

- 9時から始まります。— [It] starts from 9 o'clock.
- 家から駅まで歩きます。— [I] walk from home to the station. (see [[8b-まで]] for the から〜まで pair)
- 大阪から来ました。— [I] came from Osaka.

## Source / origin (broader than physical start)
から also marks where something originates from more abstractly — who gave/told/made something, or what something is derived from:

- 先生から聞きました。— [I] heard [it] from the teacher.
- 友達から手紙が来た。— A letter came from a friend.
- 米から作られる。— [It]'s made from rice.

Compare with に as indirect object (see [[3a-に]]): 先生に聞きました (I asked the teacher — targeting them) vs 先生から聞きました (I heard it from the teacher — them as the source). Different verbs, different direction of information flow.

## Reason/cause: "because"
Attaches to the end of a clause (after plain or polite form) to give a reason — the から-clause comes first, the result/conclusion follows:

- 疲れたから、休みます。— Because [I]'m tired, [I]'ll rest.
- 雨が降っているから、行きたくない。— Since it's raining, [I] don't want to go.
- 危ないから、気をつけて。— [It]'s dangerous, so be careful.

Unlike the particle use (which attaches only to nouns), reason-から attaches to a full clause — verb, adjective, or noun+だ/です:

- 学生だから、お金がない。— Since [I]'m a student, [I] don't have money. (plain だ before から, even in a polite conversation — see [[3-plain-vs-polite-form]])
- 忙しいですから、行けません。— [I]'m busy, so [I] can't go. (polite です before から is also fine — unlike と思う, から doesn't force plain form)

## から vs ので (reason particles — brief note)
から is more direct/assertive about the cause; ので is softer and more common in polite/formal explanations. から is the safer daily default — ので is a later refinement, not needed to start.

## Notes
- から can stack after は for contrast: 明日からは頑張ります。— Starting tomorrow, [I]'ll work hard [implying: unlike until now].
- てから is a related but distinct pattern: て-form + から means "after doing X" (sequencing, not reason) — 食べてから、寝ます。— [I]'ll sleep after eating. Don't confuse this with plain-form+から (reason).
