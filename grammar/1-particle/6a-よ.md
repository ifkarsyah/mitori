# よ - asserting new information particle

A sentence-final particle that flags what you're saying as new/important information the listener doesn't have yet — roughly "I'm telling you," "just so you know." See [[6a-ね]] for the contrasting "seeking agreement" particle and [[6c-わ]] for the softening particle.

## Asserting new info
Use よ when you're telling the listener something they likely don't already know, or correcting/updating their assumption:

- もう12時ですよ。— It's already 12, you know. (they probably didn't realize)
- これ、おいしいですよ。— This is tasty, [I'm telling you]. (recommending, they haven't tried it)
- 危ないよ! — Watch out! / That's dangerous! (urgent new info)

Compare directly with ね: おいしいですね (isn't this tasty — inviting agreement, assuming they'll agree) vs おいしいですよ (this is tasty — informing/asserting, they don't know yet).

## Correction / pushback
よ carries a mild "actually..." force when contradicting or correcting the listener:

- 違いますよ。— That's wrong, [actually].
- もう食べましたよ。— [I] already ate, [you know]. (correcting an assumption that you hadn't)

Overusing よ in this mode can come across as pushy or condescending — tone and relationship matter a lot.

## Emphasis / insistence
よ can simply add force/insistence to a statement, opinion, or invitation:

- 行きましょうよ! — Let's go [come on]!
- 大丈夫ですよ。— It's fine, [really].

## Stacking with か/ね
よ combines with other sentence-final particles:

- よね — assert + check agreement: 明日休みですよね? — Tomorrow's a holiday, right? (see [[6a-ね]])
- よ alone doesn't combine with か (they serve opposite functions — asserting vs questioning) — か replaces よ, not stacks with it.

## Notes
- Dial down よ with strangers/superiors in sensitive moments — it can sound blunt; ね or no particle at all is safer when unsure.
- よ is not inherently rude — it's just informational emphasis; tone of voice determines whether it lands as helpful or curt.
