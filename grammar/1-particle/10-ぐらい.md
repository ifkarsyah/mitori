# ぐらい / くらい - "about/approximately" particle

Marks a rough estimate — amount, time, degree — rather than an exact figure. Both spellings/pronunciations are used interchangeably (くらい is slightly more common in careful/formal speech, ぐらい in casual speech), no meaning difference.

## Approximate quantity
Attaches after a number/amount to soften it into an estimate:

- 3時間ぐらい待ちました。— [I] waited about 3 hours.
- 千円くらいです。— [It]'s about 1000 yen.
- 10人ぐらい来ました。— About 10 people came.

## Approximate degree (comparison of scale)
ぐらい/くらい can also express "to the extent of," comparing a degree or intensity rather than a quantity — often paired with a noun/clause as the reference point:

- 死ぬぐらい疲れた。— [I]'m tired to the point of [almost] dying. (hyperbolic, very common casual exaggeration)
- 彼ぐらい上手じゃない。— [I]'m not as skilled as him. (roughly "to his degree")

## それぐらい / どのぐらい — common set phrases
- それぐらい分かるよ。— [I] know [at least] that much. (mild pushback, "give me some credit")
- どのぐらいかかりますか。— About how long does [it] take? (asking for an estimate — extremely common daily question, alongside どれぐらい)

## ぐらい vs ほど — brief note
ほど is a more formal/written near-synonym for the "degree/extent" sense of ぐらい — 〜ほど not needed as a first pass; ぐらい/くらい covers casual daily use fine on its own.

## Notes
- ぐらい/くらい doesn't replace は/が/を the way だけ/しか/も do — it attaches to the quantity/degree expression itself, which then behaves normally in the sentence (3時間ぐらい待った — ぐらい sits between the quantity and the verb, no particle conflict).
- Can combine with other particles: 3時間ぐらいで着きます。— [We]'ll arrive in about 3 hours. (ぐらい + で, marking the approximate duration as a bounding scope — see [[3b-で]]).
