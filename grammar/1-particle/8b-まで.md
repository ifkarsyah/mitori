# まで - "until/up to" particle

Marks an endpoint — in time, space, or extent. Pairs naturally with [[8a-から]] to bracket a range: から marks where something starts, まで marks where it ends.

## Endpoint: time/place "until/to"
- 9時まで働きます。— [I] work until 9 o'clock.
- 駅まで歩きます。— [I] walk to/as far as the station.
- 家から駅まで10分です。— [It]'s 10 minutes from home to the station.

The から〜まで pairing is the standard way to express a range with a clear start and end:

- 月曜日から金曜日まで働きます。— [I] work from Monday to Friday.
- 東京から大阪まで新幹線で行きました。— [I] went from Tokyo to Osaka by shinkansen.

## まで alone (no から needed)
まで works fine without a stated starting point — the start is just whatever "now"/the default reference point is:

- 明日まで待ってください。— Please wait until tomorrow.
- ここまでできました。— [I]'ve gotten this far. / [I]'ve done up to here.

## まで vs までに — a critical distinction
This is the single most important thing to get right with まで: **まで** means the action/state continues *up through* the endpoint, while **までに** means the action must happen *by* (at some point no later than) the deadline — a one-time event, not a continuous state:

- 5時まで待ちます。— [I]'ll wait until 5. (waiting continues the whole time, up to 5)
- 5時までに来てください。— Please come by 5. (arrival happens at some point no later than 5 — not "arriving continuously")

Mixing these up is a common, meaning-changing mistake:
- 明日まで終わらせます。would oddly suggest "finishing" as an ongoing state through tomorrow.
- 明日までに終わらせます。— [I]'ll get [it] finished by tomorrow. (correct — finishing is a one-time event with a deadline)

Rule of thumb: if the verb describes something continuous/ongoing (待つ, 働く, いる), use plain まで. If the verb describes a one-time completed event (終わる, 出す, 来る, 提出する), use までに.

## Extent: "even/as far as" (emphatic)
Like も (see [[5b-も]]), まで can push toward an extreme, emphatic reading — "even," "to the extent of":

- 彼は私にまで嘘をついた。— He lied even to me.
- そこまで言わなくても。— [You] didn't have to go that far [in what you said]. (lit. "even to that point")

## Notes
- まで doesn't replace は/が/を the way many other particles do — it can combine with は for contrast: 5時までは大丈夫です。— Until 5, [it]'s fine [implying: after that, it's not].
- Don't confuse まで (until, this file) with 〜前に (before) — 5時まで (up until 5, inclusive of the whole span) vs 5時前に (before 5, i.e. sometime earlier than 5) describe different things.
