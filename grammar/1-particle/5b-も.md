# も - "also/too" particle

Marks something as being included alongside something else — "also," "too," or (in negative sentences) "not... either." See [[5a-と]] for how も differs from と's exhaustive-list role.

## Basic "also/too"
も replaces は/が/を on the noun it attaches to — it doesn't stack with them:

- 私も学生です。— I'm also a student. (implying someone else already was mentioned)
- コーヒーも飲みます。— [I] drink coffee too.
- 田中さんも来ました。— Tanaka came too.

Compare with と: 私と田中さん (me and Tanaka, listed together in one clause) vs 私も行きます。田中さんも行きます。(I'm going too. Tanaka's going too. — separately confirmed, additive across sentences/clauses).

## Negative "not... either"
In a negative sentence, も means "not this one either" — very common in daily conversation:

- 私も行きません。— I'm not going either.
- 肉も魚も食べません。— [I] don't eat meat or fish [either]. (see below for the も...も pattern)

## も...も — "both...and" / "neither...nor"
Repeating も across two or more items gives an exhaustive "both X and Y" (affirmative) or "neither X nor Y" (negative):

- 兄も姉もいます。— I have both an older brother and an older sister.
- 時間もお金もない。— [I] have neither time nor money.

This is a distinct, stronger construction from と — と just lists things factually, も...も emphasizes the "even this, even that" completeness.

## Emphatic extremes ("even")
も can push toward "even," especially with numbers or extreme cases:

- 三時間も待ちました。— [I] waited a whopping three hours. (emphasizing it's a lot)
- 一円も持っていない。— [I] don't have even one yen.

## Notes
- も cannot combine with は/が/を on the same noun — it takes their place.
- With other particles (に, で, と, から), も stacks after them rather than replacing them: 東京にも行きました。— [I] went to Tokyo too.
- Don't confuse the additive も (this note) with も as part of 〜てもいい (see the て-form hub in [[0-overview]]) — that's an unrelated grammaticalized use, not "also."
