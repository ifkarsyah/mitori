# ばかり - "just did X" / "only, all X" particle

Two related but distinct uses: marking something as having *just* happened, or marking something as happening *excessively/exclusively*. Different nuance from だけ/しか (see [[9a-だけ]]/[[9b-しか]]), which are about strict limits, not recency or repetition.

## Just did X — たばかり
Attaches after plain past (た-form, see [[../2-verb/2-て-form]] for the sound-shift table) to mean "just did," emphasizing how recently something happened:

- 食べたばかりです。— [I] just ate.
- 日本に来たばかりです。— [I] just came to Japan [recently].
- 始めたばかりで、まだよく分かりません。— [I] just started, so [I] don't really understand [it] yet.

This is a distinct, common daily pattern — different from さっき (a moment ago, a plain adverb) in that ばかり grammatically frames the *state of having just done it* as still relevant/fresh.

## Only/all X — excessive repetition or exclusivity
Attaches after nouns (with を often dropped) or plain form, meaning "nothing but X," "always doing X" — usually with a mildly critical or exasperated tone:

- 彼はゲームばかりしている。— He's always playing games [and nothing else]. (see [[../2-verb/9-ている]] for the ている state)
- お菓子ばかり食べないで。— Don't eat nothing but sweets. (see [[../4-pattern/2-requests]] for ないで as a casual negative request)
- 文句ばかり言う。— [He] does nothing but complain.

This use carries an implicit "...and that's a problem" undertone — different from だけ, which is neutral about the limit it states.

## ばかり vs たばかり — don't confuse the two
Same word, different attachment point, different meaning entirely:

| Form | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 動詞た形 + ばかり | just did X (recency) |
| 名詞/動詞 + ばかり | only/always X (excess, mild criticism) |

## Notes
- ばかり can combine with いる for a compound state: 泣いてばかりいる。— [She]'s always crying. (て-form + ばかり + いる)
- Casual speech often shortens ばかり to just ばっかり or ばっか — same meaning, more emphatic/colloquial: ゲームばっかり! — Nothing but games!
