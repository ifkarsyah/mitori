# か - question particle

Turns a statement into a question by attaching to the end. Also does double duty as an "or" connector between choices. See [[6a-ね]]/[[6a-よ]] for the other sentence-final particles it can precede but not stack after.

## Basic yes/no and wh-questions
Add か to the end of a plain or polite sentence to make it a question — no word-order change needed, unlike English:

- 学生ですか。— Are [you] a student?
- 何を食べますか。— What will [you] eat?
- 行きましたか。— Did [you] go?

## Politeness split
か is the standard way to ask questions in polite (です/ます) speech. In casual speech, か is often dropped entirely, replaced by rising intonation alone, or by の (see [[4-の]] "explanatory の"):

- 行きますか。(polite, explicit question marker)
- 行くの? (casual, の + rising tone)
- 行く? (casual, tone alone — か would sound oddly formal/blunt here)

Using plain-form + か in casual speech (行くか?) can sound rough/masculine — worth knowing but not a default beginners should reach for.

## "Or" between choices
か can link two or more nouns/clauses as alternatives:

- コーヒーか紅茶がほしいです。— I want coffee or tea.
- 行くか行かないか決めてください。— Please decide whether to go or not.

## Embedded questions (uncertainty inside a sentence)
か can mark an embedded question — "whether/if" — inside a larger sentence, very common with 分かる/知る/考える:

- 彼が来るかどうか分かりません。— I don't know whether he'll come. (どうか adds "or not")
- 何を食べるか決めていません。— [I] haven't decided what to eat.

This plain-form-plus-か pattern is the backbone of a lot of "I wonder / not sure if" constructions.

## Rhetorical / softening use
か can appear mid-sentence or doubled up to hedge or muse aloud, without being a real question:

- そうですか。— Is that so. / I see. (acknowledging, not actually asking)
- 誰か来ましたか。— Did someone come? (誰か = "someone," か attached to 誰 makes an indefinite pronoun — a different, unrelated job of か)

## Notes
- か replaces nothing — it just attaches to the end of an already-complete sentence.
- か doesn't combine with よ (see [[6a-よ]]) since they point opposite directions (assert vs. ask), but でしょうか is common for a softer, more polite "I wonder/is it perhaps."
- か attached to question words (誰, 何, どこ, いつ) turns them into indefinites: 誰か (someone), 何か (something), どこか (somewhere) — worth recognizing as a separate pattern from the sentence-final question use.
