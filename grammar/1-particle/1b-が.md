# が - subject/emphasis particle

Marks the grammatical subject, with a flavor of "this one specifically" — pointing at *who/what* does or is something, often as new or highlighted information. See [[1a-は]] for the full は-vs-が comparison table.

## Core pattern
**X が Y** → "X is the one that Y" / introduces X as the answer

- 猫がいます。— There's a cat. (announcing its existence)
- 誰が来ましたか。— Who came? / 田中さんが来ました。— Tanaka came.
- 雨が降っています。— It's raining. (rain as the subject of the observed event)

## New information / answering "who/what"
が is the natural choice when you're identifying something out of a group, or stating a fact for the first time:

- あの人が犯人です。— That person is the culprit. (picking them out)
- どれがあなたのですか。— Which one is yours? / これが私のです。— This one is mine.

Contrast with は, which would sound like "that person, [as for them,] is the culprit" — fine, but less natural as a first-mention answer.

## Objects of feeling/ability/desire
A large chunk of が's daily use isn't about "subject" at all — certain predicates just grammatically require が for their object:

- 日本語が分かります。— I understand Japanese.
- コーヒーが好きです。— I like coffee.
- 車がほしいです。— I want a car.
- 漢字が読めます。— I can read kanji. (potential form)

These aren't は/が contrast cases — the thing liked/wanted/understood/able-to-be-done is marked with が, full stop, even though English treats it like an object ("I like coffee" → coffee = object, but Japanese treats it as what's inducing the feeling in you).

## Subordinate clauses: が where は can't go
Inside a relative clause or subordinate clause modifying a noun, the subject is marked with が (not は), even if it would take は as a main-clause topic:

- 私が作った料理です。— This is the food [that] I made.
- 猫がいる家。— A house where there's a cat.

This is the same mechanic behind 象は鼻が長い (see [[1a-は]]) — が marks the local subject inside the descriptive part, while は sets the overall topic.

## Emphatic contrast (the opposite direction of は)
が can also emphasize "X and only X," gently excluding alternatives — often felt as slightly assertive or corrective:

- 僕がやります。— I'll do it. (I'm the one who will — not someone else)
- それが問題です。— That's the problem. (that one specifically)

## Notes
- が cannot stack with を or は on the same noun — it replaces them.
- Beginners overuse は and underuse が — if you're introducing something for the first time or answering a "who/what" question, reach for が.
