# だけ - "only" (neutral) particle

Marks a strict limit — "only X," "just X" — in either affirmative or negative sentences, with a neutral, matter-of-fact tone. See [[9b-しか]] for the negative-only counterpart with a different nuance.

## Basic "only"
Attaches directly to nouns, replacing は/が/を much like も does (see [[5b-も]]):

- 千円だけあります。— [I] only have 1000 yen. (stated as plain fact)
- これだけください。— Please give [me] just this.
- 少しだけ食べました。— [I] ate just a little.

## Works in affirmative sentences (unlike しか)
だけ is fine standing alone with a positive verb — this is the key structural difference from しか, which forces a negative verb:

- 水だけ飲みます。— [I]'ll only drink water. (plain affirmative, no negative needed)

## だけ with verbs/clauses
だけ can attach after a plain-form verb or clause, not just nouns, meaning "only [do X]" or "just because [X]":

- 見るだけです。— [I]'m just looking. (common in shops, deflecting a salesperson)
- 言っただけです。— [I] just said [it, no deeper meaning].

## Tone: neutral, not emotional
だけ simply states a limit without extra feeling — compare with しか, which carries a stronger "that's all there is, and it feels insufficient" undertone (see [[9b-しか]]). だけ is the safer, more neutral default when you just want to say "only."

## Notes
- だけ can combine with で: 一人だけで行った。— [I] went alone, just by myself.
- だけじゃなく(て) — "not only" — sets up a "not only X, but also Y" pattern: 日本語だけじゃなく、中国語も話せます。— [I] can speak not only Japanese, but also Chinese [see [[5b-も]] for the も half of this pattern].
