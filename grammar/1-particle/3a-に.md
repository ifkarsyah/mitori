# に - time / destination / indirect object particle

に marks a fixed point: a point in time, a point you move toward, a point something exists at, or the person/thing on the receiving end of an action. See [[3b-で]] for the に-vs-で comparison.

## Time
Specific points in time (clock times, dates, days of week) take に. Relative time words (今日, 明日, 毎日, etc.) usually don't:

- 7時に起きます。— I get up at 7.
- 月曜日に行きます。— I'll go on Monday.
- 2020年に生まれました。— [I] was born in 2020.
- ~~今日に行きます~~ → 今日行きます。— I'll go today. (no に)

## Destination (motion toward a point)
With 行く/来る/帰る and similar verbs, に marks where you're headed:

- 学校に行きます。— I'm going to school.
- 家に帰ります。— I'm going home.
- 東京に来ました。— [I] came to Tokyo.

へ can substitute for this use (softer, more "in the direction of"), but に is the default and also does more jobs へ can't.

## Existence / location of being
With いる/ある (existence verbs), に marks where something is located — not で:

- 猫が部屋にいます。— The cat is in the room.
- 机の上に本があります。— There's a book on the desk.

## Indirect object (the receiver)
に marks the person something is given to, said to, sent to, taught to — the "to X" of "give X to Y":

- 友達にプレゼントをあげました。— I gave a present to my friend.
- 先生に聞きました。— I asked the teacher.
- メールを田中さんに送りました。— I sent an email to Tanaka.

## Purpose (before a movement verb)
Verb stem + に + movement verb = "go/come [in order] to do":

- 昼ご飯を食べに行きます。— I'm going to eat lunch.
- 映画を見に行きました。— [I] went to see a movie.

## に vs で — the key split
Both can look like "at/in [place]," but they're not interchangeable:

| に | で |
|---|---|
| Where something **exists** (いる/ある) | Where an **action happens** |
| Fixed point in time | — |
| Destination of motion | Means/instrument of doing something |
| 部屋にいます。(I'm in the room — existing) | 部屋で勉強します。(I study in the room — acting) |

Rule of thumb: if the verb is いる/ある or a "arrive-at" verb, use に. If the verb describes an activity happening in a place, use で. See [[3b-で]] for the full breakdown from で's side.

## Notes
- に can stack after は/も for topic/contrast: 東京には行きました。(see [[1a-は]])
- に also marks the agent in passive/causative sentences (先生に叱られた — was scolded by the teacher) — long-tail, not needed early.
